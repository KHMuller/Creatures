Natural Selection with "Creatures" - A Simulation

Get a first-hand look at the process of natural selection with a simulation called "Creatures" written in JavaScript using the P5.js library. Experiment with different parameters and watch as species adapt and survive over time. For notes and explanations see below.

Random Notes

Each cycle, the whole map is processed and each creature updated using a clone of the current map. When different offsprings are trying to occupy the same spot, they crash and vanish.

Models

  1. Creatures use locC on locC' for spotting neighbors and for deciding if they are friends and foes.
  2. Creatures use locA on locA' for spotting neighbors and locC on locC' for deciding if they are friends or foes.
  3. Creatures use locA on locB' for spotting neighbors and locC on locC' for deciding if they are friends or foes.
  4. Creatures use locA on locC' for spotting neighbors and locC on locC' for deciding if they are friends or foes.

Polarization

With a positive polarization, a creature gains energy by having allies around and loses energy by having rivals around. With a negative polarization, the effect is reverse.
