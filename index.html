Natural Selection of "Creatures" - A Simulation

Get a first-hand look at the process of natural selection with this simulation called "Creatures" written in JavaScript using the P5.js library. Experiment with different models and watch as creatures adapt and survive over time. For notes and explanations see below.

Random Notes

Each cycle, the whole map is processed and each creature updated using a clone of the current map. When different offsprings are trying to occupy the same spot, they crash and vanish.

Models

There are two interactions tested between two individual creatures. First, can they see each other (perception) and secondly, tested only if they can see each other, are they friendly to each other (relationship). A model is described with two two-letter codes (e.g. CC, AB, ...). The capital letters of a code indicate which genes are used to determine perception or relationship (! indicates used to determine relationship). For example the code for Model 4 is "AA,!BC" which means that locA of individual 1 is compared to locA of individual 2 to determine perception and locB of individual 1 is compared to locC of individual 2 to determine relationship.

If test for perception passes (similar genes) then and only then relationship is tested. Based on the result of perception and relationship, an increase or decrease of fitness of individual 1 is calculated.

If an individual's fitness passes a certain threshold then it may divide if there is at least one empty field next to its location. If another individual tries to place a progeny in the same spot, the progeny with the higher fitness will get the spot.

Polarization

By default, friendly neighbors (passed relationship test) provide a fitness gain while unfriendly neighbors (failed relationship test) will cause a loss in fitness. With polarization the gain or loss can be set inverse.
